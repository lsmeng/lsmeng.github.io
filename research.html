Research

We study earthquake physics, seismic hazards, and tsunami processes by combining seismic observations, source imaging, numerical modeling, and data-driven approaches — investigating how ruptures nucleate, propagate, and interact with complex fault systems, and turning that understanding into better hazard assessment, monitoring, and early warning. A central thread of our methodological work is seismic array processing — back-projection — for earthquake source imaging. Explore the topics below.

Rupture Dynamics & Supershear Earthquakes

Imaging how ruptures propagate, branch, and reach supershear speeds — from Sumatra and Palu to Türkiye and Mandalay.

Seismic Array Source Imaging

Back-projection and MUSIC high-resolution imaging, with reference-window, slowness calibration and 3-D extensions.

Earthquake Early Warning

Small-scale seismic arrays that track rupture growth and directivity in real time for finite-source early warning.

Tsunami Prediction & Early Warning

Real-time back-projection and time-reversal imaging to forecast tsunami arrival time and coastal run-up.

Earthquake Swarms & Fluid-Driven Seismicity

How migrating fluids and multiscale fault complexity drive swarms — Noto Peninsula and the Tengchong Volcanic Field.

Pre-, Post-, and Independent Slow Slip

Foreshocks, afterslip and slow-slip transients that precede and follow great earthquakes.
